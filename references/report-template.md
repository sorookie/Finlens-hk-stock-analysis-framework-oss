# Stock Analysis Report Template

## General Rule

A strong report should let the reader understand the stock quickly, then go deeper section by section.
Start with the judgment, then show the evidence.

## Full Report Structure

### 1. Executive Summary

This section should stand on its own.
Include:

* one sentence on what the company does
* the core investment view
* the main reason the stock could work
* the main reason the thesis could fail
* the valuation or target price view
* the overall rating view such as Buy, Hold, Sell when appropriate

### 2. Company Overview

Cover:

* business description
* major segments or revenue drivers
* industry and competitive set
* market position
* listing structure when relevant
* quick statistics table

Useful quick stats:

* stock name and code
* exchange
* market cap
* current or last available price
* trading currency
* board lot size when relevant
* 52 week range
* average turnover or volume
* next results date when relevant
* market data freshness label

### 3. Investment Thesis

**Bull Case**

Include 3 to 5 strongest reasons.
For each reason, state:

* what the argument is
* what evidence supports it
* what metric or trend matters most

**Bear Case**

Include 3 to 5 main risks.
For each risk, state:

* what could go wrong
* why the market may care
* what evidence already hints at the risk

### 4. Fundamental Analysis

Cover these blocks:

**Business Quality**
* moat
* management quality
* business model durability

**Financial Performance**
* revenue trend
* margin trend
* profit trend
* balance sheet strength
* cash flow profile

**Growth Quality**
* historical growth
* segment drivers
* future growth levers
* how credible that growth looks

**Shareholder Return Quality**
* dividend support
* payout quality
* buyback discipline
* whether capital return is backed by real cash generation

### 5. Valuation Analysis

**Current Valuation Table**

Include metrics such as:

* trailing P/E
* forward P/E
* PEG
* P/B when relevant
* EV/EBITDA when relevant
* EV/Sales when relevant
* FCF yield when relevant
* dividend yield
* total shareholder yield when relevant

**Valuation Framing**

Explain:

* how current valuation compares with history
* how it compares with peers
* what assumptions are embedded in the current multiple
* whether yield or buybacks create real support
* fair value range and the basis for that range

### 6. Ownership and Positioning Analysis

Cover when relevant:

* southbound holding trend
* daily short-selling turnover and ratio
* weekly aggregated reportable short positions trend
* buyback activity as a support or absorption factor
* optional CCASS participant concentration and recent changes when a local connector is enabled
* whether positioning looks supportive, neutral, crowded, or deteriorating

### 7. Technical Analysis

Include only when useful or requested.

Cover:

* trend direction
* moving average position
* RSI and MACD summary
* volume behavior
* support and resistance
* chart pattern if clearly present
* short term and medium term technical outlook
* liquidity warning if chart reliability is weak
* freshness label for the technical input data

### 8. Risks

Split into:

**Company Specific Risks**
* execution risk
* product concentration
* demand slowdown
* regulatory or policy risk
* capital allocation mistakes

**Market and External Risks**
* rate sensitivity
* macro slowdown
* commodity swing when relevant
* FX exposure when relevant
* multiple compression
* southbound reversal or liquidity withdrawal when relevant
* positioning unwind or short pressure when relevant

### 9. Catalysts and Monitoring Timeline

List what could move the stock next.

**Potential Positive Catalysts**
* results beat
* guidance revision
* margin inflection
* stronger dividend or buyback
* southbound accumulation
* positioning de-crowding or short covering
* policy easing when relevant

**Potential Negative Catalysts**
* miss or guide down
* dividend cut or weak payout visibility
* margin compression
* demand reset
* policy pressure
* crowded positioning unwind
* rising short inventory

### 10. Recommendation

When a recommendation is appropriate, state:

* rating view
* conviction level
* fair value range or target price
* timeframe
* key assumptions
* what would make you upgrade the view
* what would make you cut or reverse the view

### 11. Conclusion

Close with:

* the most important 3 to 4 takeaways
* the current stance
* the next data points to watch

## Comparison Report Structure

For stock versus stock comparisons, use this structure.

### 1. Side by Side Overview Table

Compare:

* business focus
* market cap
* revenue scale
* growth
* margins
* valuation
* balance sheet quality
* dividend and buyback support
* ownership and positioning context when relevant
* recent price performance if relevant

### 2. Business and Quality Comparison

Explain where each company is stronger.
Possible angles:

* moat
* product mix
* cyclicality
* pricing power
* management execution

### 3. Financial and Valuation Comparison

Use tables where possible.
Compare:

* growth
* profitability
* free cash flow
* leverage
* valuation multiples
* dividend quality
* buyback strength

### 4. Ownership and Positioning Comparison

Compare when relevant:

* southbound trend
* short-selling flow
* weekly short inventory trend
* buyback support
* optional participant concentration when available

### 5. Technical Comparison

Compare:

* trend quality
* relative strength
* support resistance position
* momentum condition
* liquidity quality

### 6. Verdict

State:

* which name looks more attractive now
* why
* for what type of investor or setup
* what would change the conclusion

## Writing Rules

* keep the opening sharp
* do not bury the conclusion
* use tables for metrics and comparisons
* keep numbers labeled with dates or periods
* state currency when relevant
* keep market data separate from filings and ownership data
* always label freshness for non live price data
