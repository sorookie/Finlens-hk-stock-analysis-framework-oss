# Technical Analysis Reference

## Objective

Use chart structure and indicators to understand trend, momentum, support resistance, and potential trade location.
Technical analysis should support disciplined interpretation, not false precision.

## Timeframe Discipline

Use the timeframe that matches the question:

* **Weekly chart** for primary trend and major structure
* **Daily chart** for most swing analysis and support resistance work
* **Intraday chart** only if the user explicitly wants short term trading context

Do not mix timeframes carelessly.
A daily pullback inside a weekly uptrend is not the same as a broken primary trend.

## Core Workflow

1. Identify the primary trend first
2. Mark major support and resistance
3. Check price relative to key moving averages
4. Review momentum indicators such as RSI and MACD
5. Confirm or reject the setup with volume
6. Check whether turnover and liquidity are healthy enough for chart signals to matter
7. Identify whether a real chart pattern exists
8. Define the levels that confirm the thesis and the levels that invalidate it

## Key Indicators

### Trend Indicators

**Moving Averages**
* SMA = Simple Moving Average
* EMA = Exponential Moving Average
* Common references: 20 day, 50 day, 200 day
* Above rising 50 day and 200 day often supports an uptrend read
* Below falling 50 day and 200 day often supports a downtrend read
* Golden Cross: 50 day moving above 200 day
* Death Cross: 50 day moving below 200 day

**MACD**
* MACD Line = 12 EMA - 26 EMA
* Signal Line = 9 EMA of MACD
* Histogram = MACD - Signal
* Bullish when MACD crosses above signal line with improving momentum
* Bearish when MACD crosses below signal line with weakening momentum

### Momentum Indicators

**RSI**
* Range: 0 to 100
* Above 70 often means overbought conditions
* Below 30 often means oversold conditions
* In strong trends, RSI can stay elevated or depressed longer than expected
* Divergence matters when price makes a new high or low and RSI does not confirm

**Stochastic Oscillator**
* Range: 0 to 100
* Above 80 often means overbought
* Below 20 often means oversold
* Use as a supporting signal, not a standalone thesis

### Volatility Indicators

**Bollinger Bands**
* Middle Band = 20 day SMA
* Upper Band = Middle + 2 standard deviations
* Lower Band = Middle - 2 standard deviations
* Band squeeze can precede expansion
* Expansion often signals rising realized volatility

**ATR**
* Average True Range measures volatility
* Useful for position sizing and stop placement
* Higher ATR means price swings are larger

### Volume Indicators

**OBV**
* On Balance Volume tracks cumulative volume flow
* Rising OBV with rising price supports the uptrend
* Divergence can warn of exhaustion

**Volume Relative to Average**
* Breakouts with higher than normal volume are more credible
* Weak volume on a breakout raises failure risk

## Support and Resistance

Identify levels from:

* prior highs and lows
* repeated rejection zones
* moving averages
* earnings gap boundaries
* round numbers
* Fibonacci retracement levels when helpful

Treat support and resistance as zones, not single perfect lines.

## Chart Patterns

### Reversal Patterns

* head and shoulders
* inverse head and shoulders
* double top
* double bottom

### Continuation Patterns

* flags
* pennants
* ascending triangle
* descending triangle
* symmetrical triangle
* rectangle or range continuation

Only call a pattern if the structure is reasonably clear.
Do not force pattern labeling when price is simply noisy.

## Hong Kong Market Context Rules

1. Thin turnover can make technical signals less reliable
2. Board lot size can influence retail participation but does not itself validate the chart
3. Yield or policy names can trade more on sentiment and flow than on clean momentum structure
4. Southbound flow can reinforce trend persistence in larger names when it is strong and sustained
5. Be extra careful with small caps, low liquidity names, and sudden gap moves after announcements

## Interpretation Rules

1. Start with trend, not with indicators
2. Confirm with multiple pieces of evidence
3. Use volume to validate important moves when possible
4. Distinguish observed setup from forecasted outcome
5. Respect earnings gaps and event risk
6. Always state invalidation levels if giving a directional technical view

## Practical Readout Template

When writing a technical section, cover:

* current trend
* price versus 20 day, 50 day, and 200 day averages
* RSI status
* MACD status
* volume confirmation or divergence
* key support levels
* key resistance levels
* short term view
* medium term view
* what would turn the setup bullish or bearish
* whether liquidity conditions weaken the reliability of the read
