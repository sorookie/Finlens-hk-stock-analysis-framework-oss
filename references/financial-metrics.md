# Financial Metrics Reference

## How to Use This File

Use these metrics to interpret business quality, valuation, financial strength, cash flow quality, and shareholder return quality.
Do not use every metric every time.
Choose metrics that match the company’s business model, maturity, and sector.

## Profitability Metrics

**Gross Profit Margin**
* Formula: (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100
* Use for: pricing power, mix quality, and cost structure
* Read with: historical trend and peer comparison

**Operating Margin**
* Formula: Operating Profit / Revenue × 100
* Use for: operating efficiency and leverage in the model
* Watch for: margin expansion driven by sustainable scale versus temporary cost cuts

**Net Profit Margin**
* Formula: Net Profit / Revenue × 100
* Use for: bottom line profitability after all expenses
* Watch for: one offs, fair value gains, asset disposals, and non operating noise

**Return on Equity (ROE)**
* Formula: Net Profit / Shareholders' Equity × 100
* Use for: profitability relative to shareholder capital
* Read carefully when: leverage, low equity, or major buybacks distort the ratio

**Return on Assets (ROA)**
* Formula: Net Profit / Total Assets × 100
* Use for: asset efficiency

**Return on Invested Capital (ROIC)**
* Formula: NOPAT / Invested Capital × 100
* Use for: quality of value creation across debt and equity capital

## Valuation Metrics

**Price to Earnings (P/E)**
* Formula: Stock Price / Earnings Per Share
* Use trailing P/E for what has happened
* Use forward P/E for what the market expects
* Interpret with growth, margin durability, and quality

**PEG Ratio**
* Formula: P/E / Earnings Growth Rate
* Use for: checking whether a high multiple is supported by growth
* Be careful: cyclical or low quality earnings make PEG less reliable

**Price to Book (P/B)**
* Formula: Stock Price / Book Value Per Share
* Useful for: banks, insurers, exchanges, and some asset heavy businesses
* Less useful for: asset light platform or consumer brand companies

**Price to Sales (P/S)**
* Formula: Market Cap / Revenue
* Useful for: low profit or fast growth companies
* Must be read together with gross margin and eventual margin potential

**Enterprise Value (EV)**
* Formula: Market Cap + Total Debt - Cash
* Use for: capital structure neutral analysis

**EV/EBITDA**
* Formula: Enterprise Value / EBITDA
* Useful for: comparing operating businesses with different financing structures
* Less useful when depreciation is economically meaningful or EBITDA quality is poor

**Dividend Yield**
* Formula: Annual Dividend Per Share / Stock Price × 100
* Use for: income profile and shareholder return mix
* Never judge by yield alone

**FCF Yield**
* Formula: Free Cash Flow Per Share / Stock Price × 100
* Use for: cash earnings valuation
* Often more informative than dividend yield for mature cash generators

## Growth Metrics

**Revenue Growth**
* Formula: (Current Revenue - Prior Revenue) / Prior Revenue × 100
* Use both YoY and multi year trend

**Profit Growth**
* Formula: (Current Profit - Prior Profit) / Prior Profit × 100
* Check whether it comes from real operating improvement, price cycle, lower tax, or one offs

**Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR)**
* Formula: (Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/Years) - 1
* Use for: smoothing multi year growth history

## Liquidity Metrics

**Current Ratio**
* Formula: Current Assets / Current Liabilities
* Use for: short term liquidity check

**Quick Ratio**
* Formula: (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities
* Use for: more conservative liquidity check

**Cash Ratio**
* Formula: Cash and Cash Equivalents / Current Liabilities
* Use for: strictest liquidity view

## Leverage Metrics

**Debt to Equity**
* Formula: Total Debt / Total Equity
* Use for: leverage profile
* Compare to peers because acceptable levels vary by sector

**Net Debt**
* Formula: Total Debt - Cash and Cash Equivalents
* Use for: real leverage after cash offset

**Interest Coverage**
* Formula: EBIT / Interest Expense
* Use for: debt service capacity

## Efficiency Metrics

**Asset Turnover**
* Formula: Revenue / Average Total Assets
* Use for: asset efficiency and business model intensity

**Inventory Turnover**
* Formula: COGS / Average Inventory
* Use for: inventory discipline and demand quality in physical goods businesses

**Receivables Turnover**
* Formula: Revenue / Average Accounts Receivable
* DSO = 365 / Receivables Turnover
* Use for: cash collection quality

## Cash Flow Metrics

**Operating Cash Flow**
* Use for: cash generated by operations before capital investment

**Free Cash Flow (FCF)**
* Formula: Operating Cash Flow - Capital Expenditures
* Use for: cash available for reinvestment, debt paydown, buybacks, or dividends

**Cash Conversion Rate**
* Formula: Free Cash Flow / Net Profit
* Use for: earnings quality
* Weak conversion can signal working capital stress, heavy capex, or weak earnings quality

## Shareholder Return Metrics

**Dividend Payout Ratio**
* Formula: Dividends / Net Profit
* Use for: whether the dividend burden is sustainable

**Buyback Yield**
* Formula: Shares Repurchased Over Period / Market Cap
* Use for: whether buybacks are large enough to matter

**Total Shareholder Yield**
* Formula: Dividend Yield + Buyback Yield
* Use for: combined cash return to shareholders

## Trading Context Metrics

**Board Lot Entry Cost**
* Formula: Share Price × Board Lot Size
* Use for: practical entry threshold and retail tradability context

**Turnover Quality**
* Use for: whether price action is supported by healthy liquidity

**Southbound Holding Trend**
* Use for: whether Stock Connect ownership is rising, flat, or falling when relevant

## Interpretation Rules

1. Compare like with like
   * same period
   * same basis
   * same currency where possible
   * same accounting framing where possible

2. Do not judge a metric in isolation
   * high yield can mean income support or distress
   * low P/E can mean undervaluation or low quality earnings

3. Use the metric that fits the business
   * banks and insurers often need P/B and capital ratios
   * mature yield names often need dividend yield and FCF yield
   * fast growers may need P/S or EV/Sales plus margin trajectory

4. Follow the bridge from accounting profit to cash flow
   * many weak theses look fine on headline profit and break on cash conversion

5. Check whether capital return is funded by real cash generation
   * dividend and buyback quality matters as much as the headline yield
